## 1. Setup & scope
- Continues **"Digestive 2"** (the oral cavity, mechanical digestion, and dental formula were covered previously).
- The professor: **everything discussed in class is fair game**; expect lab-practical structure ID + short-answer physiology.

## 2. Accessory organs of the head 🎯
- **Tongue** — manipulates food, forms the **bolus**.
- **Teeth** — **mastication**.
- **Salivary glands** — three types (below).

## 3. The three salivary glands 🎯 (identify macro + micro)
- **Parotid** (largest) → watery, **enzyme-rich** saliva (salivary amylase + antimicrobial enzymes); **serous cells** are **dark-staining**.
- **Sublingual** → **mucus-rich** saliva that coats the bolus; **mucus cells** are pale/translucent.
- **Submandibular** → **mixed** (watery, enzyme-rich, and mucous).
- Amylase **always** targets **carbohydrates** → a cracker turns sweet in the mouth.

## 4. Esophagus 🎯
- **Purely peristaltic organ** — moves food throat → stomach; **no digestion**.
- **Peristalsis** = adjacent smooth-muscle layers contract rhythmically to push material A → B.
- **Histology:** **non-keratinized stratified squamous epithelium** — tough (strength + protection), moist (unlike skin).
- ~10 in; sits **posterior to the trachea**; passes the diaphragm at the **esophageal hiatus**.

## 5. Sphincters — the lower esophageal (cardiac) sphincter 🎯
- Sphincters are a **major exam theme**.
- **LES / cardiac sphincter** regulates esophagus ↔ stomach; **relaxed while swallowing, contracted otherwise**.
- Failure → reflux → **GERD**; persistent GERD → **Barrett's esophagus** → precursor to **esophageal cancer**.
- Worse at night (lying flat + peak nighttime acid). Risk: obesity, pregnancy, stress, genetics.

## 6. GI-tract wall — the 4 layers 🎯
- **Mucosa** (focus) → **submucosa** (glands) → **muscularis externa** (peristalsis/churning) → **serosa**.

## 7. Stomach — gross anatomy & function 🎯
- **J-shaped**; **lesser/greater curvatures**; regions **fundus, cardiac, pyloric**.
- **Three** muscularis-externa layers → **churning** (mechanical digestion) → mixes food with **HCl** + enzymes → **chyme**.
- **Rugae (gastric folds)** → **stretch/expand** (fist → liters).
- **HCl denatures proteins**; **pepsin** then digests them. Chemical protein digestion **begins here**.
- **NOT absorption** — the stomach **stores** and **prepares** food. Absorbed items (**aspirin, alcohol**) = **gastric irritants**.

## 8. Pyloric sphincter & gastric emptying 🎯
- Meters chyme into the duodenum **one "squirt" at a time**.
- **Pyloric stenosis** = narrowing (common in newborns) → stomach can't empty.

## 9. Stomach histology — the gastric cells 🎯 (the "10-point" image)
- **Mucus (neck) cells** → bicarbonate-rich **alkaline mucus** (protects the lining). *(Don't separate surface vs neck — ⛔.)*
- **Parietal cells** → **HCl**.
- **Chief cells** → **pepsinogen** (→ pepsin in the presence of HCl).
- **G cells** → **gastrin**.
- Digestive enzymes are released **inactive** and activate only in HCl.

## 10. Gastric ulcers 🎯
- Always **HCl eroding the lining** because of **too little mucus**.
- Causes: **H. pylori** (blocks mucus secretion), **stress** (SNS ↓ mucus), **NSAIDs/aspirin** (↓ mucus). *(Prostaglandin mechanism ⛔.)*

## 11. Basal gastric acid secretion 🎯
- **Circadian**: lowest AM, **highest at night**; continues during **interdigestive periods**.

## 12. Secretagogues & the proton pump 🎯
- **Secretagogue** = triggers another cell to secrete. Three for HCl on **parietal cells**:
  - **ACh** (vagus/PSNS; **M3**), **Gastrin** (G cells; **CCK-B**), **Histamine** (local; **H2**).
- All → **GPCR → cAMP →** the **H⁺/K⁺ ATPase (proton pump)** moves to the membrane → pumps **H⁺** (Cl⁻ follows) → **HCl**.
- Drugs: **PPIs** (omeprazole/pantoprazole) block the **pump**; **H₂ blockers** (Tagamet) block **histamine H2** — both treat **GERD**.

## 13. Inhibition of acid + the three phases 🎯
- **Somatostatin** (gastric **D cells**) = primary inhibitor. **Enterogastrones** (secretin, CCK, GIP, +GLP-1) all slow the stomach.
- **Alkaline tide**: H⁺ into stomach ↔ **HCO₃⁻ into blood** → blood leaving the stomach is **alkaline**. **Vomiting → alkalosis; diarrhea → acidosis.**
- **Three digestive phases:**
  - **Cephalic** (anticipatory; sight/smell/thought → vagus preps mucus/chief/parietal cells).
  - **Gastric** (stretch reinforces vagus; G cells → gastrin → more HCl; churning → chyme).
  - **Intestinal** (chyme in duodenum; the small intestine **coordinates all digestive organs** — weighted heaviest).
- ⛔ **Migrating motility complex / interstitial cells of Cajal** (the "stomach growl") — *not on the exam.*

## 14. Intestinal-phase hormones 🎯 (know each target)
- **Secretin** (← acid, S cells): **stomach** slow + **pancreas** **bicarbonate**.
- **CCK** (← fats/proteins): **stomach** slow + **pancreas** **enzymes** + **gallbladder** contract (bile → emulsify fat). *Broadest — 3 targets.*
- **GIP** (← carbs): **stomach** + **pancreas**.
- **GLP-1** (← carbs): slows gastric emptying (Ozempic/Wegovy) + **β-cells → insulin**.

## 15. The pancreas 🎯
- **Endocrine** = **islets of Langerhans** → insulin/glucagon to blood.
- **Exocrine** = **acinar cells → enzymes** (CCK) + **duct cells → NaHCO₃** (secretin).
- **Zymogen activation**: **enteropeptidase** → trypsinogen → **trypsin** → activates the rest.
- **Duct junction (lab ID):** common **bile duct** + **main pancreatic duct** → **hepatopancreatic ampulla (of Vater)** → **sphincter of Oddi** → **major duodenal papilla** into the duodenum.

## 16. The liver 🎯
- **Bile** (emulsifies fat), **hepatic portal circulation** (filters GI blood; Kupffer cells), **stores glycogen**; metabolism, detox, synthesis (albumin, clotting factors), bilirubin, vitamin/iron storage.
- **Macro:** lobes, falciform ligament, ligamentum teres, porta hepatis. **Micro:** **lobule**, **central vein**, **portal triad**, **sinusoids**, **hepatocyte plates**, **bile canaliculi**.

## 17. The gallbladder 🎯
- **Stores/concentrates bile**; released by **CCK**; concentration → **gallstones**.

## 18. Small intestine — structure & absorption 🎯
- **Duodenum → jejunum → ileum.**
- Surface area: **plicae circulares → villi (capillary + lacteal) → microvilli (brush border)**.
- **Carbs:** monosaccharides via **SGLT/GLUT5 → GLUT2**. **Proteins:** endo/exopeptidases; Na⁺/H⁺ cotransport; transcytosis → liver. **Fats:** bile salts → **micelles** → **chylomicrons** → **lacteal**.
- **SIBO** — colon bacteria in the ileum → malabsorption, bloating.

## 19. Large intestine & microbiome 🎯
- **Water & electrolyte absorption** (primary role); minimal, bacteria-assisted digestion.
- Anatomy: **cecum → appendix → colon → rectum → anal canal**; the **gut microbiome** aids digestion, immunity, metabolism.

## 20. Defecation reflex 🎯
- Rectal **stretch → spinal (PSNS) reflex** → **internal anal sphincter (smooth, involuntary) relaxes**; **external anal sphincter (skeletal, voluntary)** gives conscious control.
