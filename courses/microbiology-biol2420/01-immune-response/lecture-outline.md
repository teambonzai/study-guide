# Lecture Outline — Immune Response & Complement

A sequential outline of the whole lecture, in the order the professor taught it (transcript +
slides). Use it to follow the recording or review the flow. **🎯 = testable · ⛔ = not tested.**

---

## 1. Introduction
- The immune system is the last major physiology/microbiology topic before the infectious-process unit.
- Immunology is taught here from a **microbiological** perspective (A&P covers the physiology side).

## 2. What immunology is 🎯
- Study of the body's **2nd and 3rd lines of defense**.
- A healthy (**immunocompetent**) immune system: **surveys** the body → **recognizes** foreign material → **tags/destroys** it.

## 3. Self vs. non-self 🎯
- WBCs read **markers on cell surfaces** to tell self from non-self.
- **Every cell carries self markers except red blood cells** → this is why blood can be transfused.
- **Non-self = antigen.**
- Infections only win when the body is **overwhelmed** (stress, second infection, cancer, immunosuppression).

## 4. Antigens, antibodies, titers 🎯
- **Antigen (Ag / "AN")** = non-self. **Antibody (Ab / "AB")** = protein the body makes against it.
- 5 antibody classes → mnemonic **MADGE** (IgM, A, D, G, E); Greek: mu, gamma, epsilon, alpha, delta.
- **Titer** = antibody level; used to prove immunity (nursing/dental/surg-tech program entry).
- The professor's pro-vaccine stance: build immunity from weakened/partial agents rather than the full infection.

## 5. How the body differentiates self — T4 helper + MHC 🎯
- Two things work **together**: the **T4 (CD4) helper** and the **MHC**.
- **MHC Class I** — DNA/genes that place **self markers** on every cell (except RBCs).
- **MHC Class II** — on **antigen-presenting cells (APCs)**.
- ⛔ MHC Class III & IV — not covered.

## 6. Two arms of the immune system 🎯
- **B lymphocytes** → **humoral** response (antibodies).
- **T lymphocytes** → **cell-mediated** response.

## 7. Antigen–antibody binding & the epitope 🎯
- **Epitope** = the surface marker an antibody binds. (Professor: "be sure you know this word.")
- Antibodies are **Y-shaped**; the Y-tips bind epitopes.
- **IgM** (biggest, appears early) can **agglutinate** (clump) microbes for easier destruction.

## 8. Where lymphocytes come from 🎯
- All start as **stem cells in bone marrow**.
- Mature in **B**one marrow → **B cells**; travel to the **T**hymus → **T cells**.
- ⛔ History aside: B cells were once thought "bursa-derived" — wrong, but both start with "B."

## 9. Humoral vs. cell-mediated 🎯
- **Humoral** (B cells → plasma cells → antibodies): vs. **extracellular** pathogens (bacteria, toxins, circulating viruses).
- **Cell-mediated** (T cells + **cytokines**): vs. **intracellular** pathogens (viruses, fungi, intracellular bacteria), **tumors, grafts**.
- **Grafts/transplants** are non-self → need lifelong **immunosuppressants**.
- **Immunoprivileged sites** (graftable, no rejection): **upper skin, lens, cornea**.

## 10. Blood components ⛔
- Erythrocyte/platelet functions and **staining** are **not tested** ("that's an A&P thing").

## 11. T lymphocytes in detail 🎯
- **T helper (T4/CD4)** — recognition; the **sentinel**; the **only** cell that turns the response **on**.
- **T cytolytic** — inactivation (destroys infected cells).
- **T suppressor** — regulation; brings T-cell numbers back down after infection.
- **HIV/AIDS** destroys the CD4 helper → the whole response can't activate.

## 12. The immune response in the very young
- Newborns lack adaptive immunity → rely on the **cell-mediated** response (why a child's WBC count can spike dramatically, e.g., whooping cough).

## 13. Key white blood cells 🎯
- **Neutrophils → bacteria.** **Eosinophils → parasites & allergies.** **Lymphocytes → viruses.**
- Both neutrophils and eosinophils can **phagocytize** and undergo **diapedesis**.
- **Diapedesis** = WBCs squeezing out of vessels into tissues to fight infection.

## 14. Phagocytosis — CAKE 🎯
- **C**hemotaxis (drawn to microbe) → **A**dherence (latch on) → **I**ngestion (into a **phagosome**) → **K**illing (**phagosome + lysosome → phagolysosome**) → **E**limination (debris cleared; brief protein in urine possible).

## 15. APCs turn on the system (MHC II) 🎯
- After killing, a phagocyte can **display a piece** of the microbe on its surface → becomes an **APC**.
- The **T4 helper** reads the display → sends **cytokines** → recruits the rest of the immune system.

## 16. Parasites & ADCC 🎯 (know the image)
- Big parasites (**helminths/worms**) are **too big to engulf** → attacked **from the outside** by eosinophils + macrophages.
- Weapons in the image: **cytokines** (blue, recruit) · **lytic enzymes** (red, break surface) · **perforin** (yellow, punch holes).
- **Eosinophilia** = sign of a **worm** infestation. Example: **elephantiasis** (worm: *Wuchereria bancrofti*).

## 17. NETs 🎯
- **Neutrophil Extracellular Traps** — fiber "webs" that trap and kill microbes (non-phagocytic killing).

## 18. Complement 🎯 (keep it simple)
- A **cascade of proteins** that: **(1) lyses microbes, (2) neutralizes toxins/viruses, (3) enhances phagocytosis.** That's the testable version.
- Context only: **MAC** (Membrane Attack Complex) forms pores that burst pathogens; 3 pathways (classical, lectin, alternative). ⛔ deeper chemistry not tested.

## 19. Memory & cytokines 🎯
- **Memory** lives in T lymphocytes and immunoglobulins.
- **Cytokines** recruit/activate more immune cells (innate + adaptive). Aside: cancer immunotherapy (using a virus to trigger the response against tumor cells).

## 20. Humoral immunity & the 5 antibodies 🎯
- Antibodies are **specific**; they **agglutinate, neutralize, destroy,** or **opsonize** (coat for phagocytosis).
- **IgM** — early bird; first; too big to cross the placenta; short-lived; agglutinates.
- **IgG** — most abundant (~80%); "for life"; **crosses the placenta** (mom → fetus).
- **IgA** — mucous membranes & secretions (saliva, tears, breast milk). (Professor has **IgA nephropathy**.)
- **IgD** — on the B-cell surface; helps initiate the response; role not fully understood.
- **IgE** — allergy & parasite antibody; triggers **mast cells → histamine**; can cause **anaphylaxis**.

## 21. Anaphylaxis 🎯
- Severe **IgE** allergic reaction: airway + vessel constriction → **hypoxia**; can be fatal in minutes.
- Treated with an **EpiPen** (epinephrine). Common triggers: peanuts, bee/ant stings.

## 22. Immunoglobulin comparison chart ⛔
- The numbers table (molecular weight, %, half-life) is **not tested** — know each antibody's **job**.

## 23. Primary vs. secondary (anamnestic) response 🎯
- **Primary** (first exposure — infection or vaccine): **IgM first**, then **IgG rises and stays**.
- **Secondary / anamnestic** (re-exposure): fast, **huge IgG burst** from memory → you are **immune**.
- **"Immune"** = re-infection is **milder and shorter**, not "never again."
- Vaccines: **side effects** (soreness, mild fever) = normal immune response ≠ **adverse reactions** (hives, anaphylaxis, blood clots, Guillain-Barré).

## 24. Acquired immunity — the 4 types 🎯
- Requires: recognize non-self · antigen **specificity** · ability to build **memory**.
- **Natural active** — catch the infection → make IgG (**long-term / for life**).
- **Natural passive** — mom's antibodies via **placenta** + **colostrum/breast milk** (short-term).
- **Artificial active** — a **vaccine** → make your own antibodies (long-term).
- **Artificial passive** — **gamma globulin, convalescent plasma, antivenom/antitoxin** (short-term, ~6 wks–3 mo).

## 25. Passive-immunity agents 🎯
- **Gamma globulin** — pooled IgG from **thousands of donors**; best **regionally**; IM/IV; ~6 wks–3 mo.
- **Convalescent plasma** — IgG-rich plasma from **recovered** patients (COVID example).
- **Antivenom / antitoxin** — venom injected into **sheep/goats** → their **IgG** is filtered out and given (snakebite).

## 26. Vaccines — attenuated vs inactivated 🎯
- **Live-attenuated** = weakened but alive (**Sabin** polio); **risk: reversion to virulence**.
- **Inactivated** = dead/inactive (**Salk** polio); safe.

## 27. The 5 vaccine platforms 🎯 (professor: "you'll see this on the exam")
- **mRNA/DNA** (Pfizer/Moderna) · **Live-attenuated** · **Inactivated** · **Subunit** (a piece, e.g. flagella) · **Viral vector** (harmless adenovirus delivers the gene — J&J, Ebola).

## 28. Adjuvant & a good vaccine 🎯
- **Adjuvant** = added irritant (**usually a salt**) → draws in more WBCs → **stronger response**. *(Professor: "be sure you know what that is.")*
- **Good vaccine:** low toxicity · protects vs the wild pathogen · triggers **both B and T** responses · long-term memory · few boosters · cheap · long shelf life / easy to give.

## 29. Herd immunity & thimerosal 🎯
- **Herd immunity** — ~**70–80%** immune stops spread, protecting the unvaccinated (from ranchers/anthrax).
- **Thimerosal** — mercury **protein stabilizer** in **multi-dose vials**; the Wakefield MMR-autism study was **fraudulent and retracted** — **no vaccine–autism link**; single-dose syringes never had it.
- **Nosode** — consuming/injecting live, virulent secretions to "immunize"; dangerous, not a vaccine.
