## A couple of terminology clarifications from the images

- **Slide 45 is titled "Chlorhexidine."** The bullet describes it as a "complex organic base
  containing chlorine and two phenolic rings." Follow the slide's own label — the deck is grouping
  it with the phenol/chlorine-type agents. *(Chemically, chlorhexidine is a bisbiguanide; the deck's
  "two phenolic rings" describes the chlorophenyl end groups. Cited as an aside — study it as the
  slide presents it.)*
- **Slide 30** ("Food products / Medical products") is the **uses** list for the radiation section
  that begins on Slide 29 — ionizing radiation sterilizes packaged food and single-use medical
  supplies.
