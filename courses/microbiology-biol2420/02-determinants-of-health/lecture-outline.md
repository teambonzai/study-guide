## 1. Unit 4 opener
- "Determinants of health and disease — from a **microbiological** perspective."
- The professor's pitch for lifelong learning (he attends a weekly class himself).

## 2. What determines good health 🎯
- Three overlapping pieces: **physical**, **mental**, **spiritual** health.
- Spiritual ≠ religious — it's having a **purpose**; a hopeful patient's body helps fight disease.

## 3. What affects health — the determinants 🎯
- **Social determinants** (income, education, employment, environment, healthcare access, etc.).
- **Pyramid of impact:** **socioeconomic factors = largest impact**; counseling/education = smallest.
- **Complex-disease wheel:** genetics · environment · host · social · **epigenetics**.
- His thesis: the biggest lever is **education** — helping people understand *why* to follow a care plan.
- Example: COVID hit hardest those who **couldn't work from home** (skill-set/exposure link).

## 4. Pathogenic, opportunistic, virulent 🎯
- **Pathogenic** = ability to cause disease. **Opportunistic** = harmless until the host/site is wrong.
- **Virulent** = high degree of pathogenicity.
- **Worked example:** *E. coli* O157:H7 vs. gut *E. coli* — both pathogenic; **O157:H7 more virulent**
  (~20% get **hemolytic uremic syndrome**, ~20% mortality).

## 5. Portals of entry 🎯
- Site where a microbe enters: cuts, lesions, injection sites, natural orifices.
- **Four pathways:** **skin · mucous membranes · placenta · parenteral** (IV/shared needles).
- Opioid crisis → shared-needle HIV/hepatitis spread (parenteral).

## 6. Contamination vs. infection 🎯
- **Contamination** = microbe merely present (skin, cutting board, water). Objects are contaminated, not infected.
- **Infection** = organism evades defenses, multiplies, becomes established, and acts on tissue.

## 7. Skin & mouth as portals ⛔(detail) / 🎯(concept)
- Skin: great barrier while intact; infections can be superficial → full-thickness.
- Mouth: how we interact with the world (eat, smell, taste); many things get in.
- ⛔ **Mother → fetus transmission table** *(Slide 12)* — *"you will not be responsible for this table."*

## 8. Disease vs. infection 🎯
- **Disease** = harmful alteration of tissue/metabolism — no microbe required (CF, cancer, diabetes, CHD,
  depression, **alcoholism**, lupus). Tissue = arthritis; metabolic = diabetes/hyperglycemia.
- **Infections** end in **-itis** (inflammation from a microbe): cellulitis, pharyngitis, cystitis, etc.

## 9. Etiology & disease-classification terms 🎯
- **Etiology** = study of cause. **Germ theory**; **Koch's postulates** (prove a microbe causes a disease).
- Know **immunological · neoplastic · iatrogenic · idiopathic · nosocomial** ("starting with immunological
  and going down").
- Modernize nosocomial → **HAI** (healthcare-associated) and **CAI** (community-acquired).
- ⛔ heredity · congenital · degenerative · nutritional · endocrine · mental — *"outside the scope."*
- ⛔ medical-terminology prefixes/suffixes slide — *"not going to ask about this"* (but take a med-term course!).

## 10. The infectious process 🎯
- **Infection** = entry, manifestation, growth, proliferation within a host.
- **3 requirements:** enter host tissues · establish & grow · obtain nutrients. Block one → no infection.
- Ties to "control of microbes": antibiotics halt growth → halt infection. HIV needs the T4 helper as its nutrient source.

## 11. Infectious agents → cancer 🎯
- **HPV → cervical** (+ anal/oral); **H. pylori → gastric**; **Hepatitis B → liver.**
- These are **risk factors**; long latency → we **screen** (Pap smear). Some carry **oncogenes** from birth.

## 12. Infection vs. intoxication 🎯
- **Intoxication** (toxin) = short, sharp, **no fever** (*S. aureus* food poisoning: ~4 h, ~24 h, vomiting).
- **Infection** (organism grows) = long, **with fever** (*Salmonella*: 3–14 d, until treated, cramps/fever/
  diarrhea/dehydration/**tenesmus**). Dx: **stool sample** → antibiotics.
- **Fever is the giveaway** of a bacterial infection. **Typhoid Mary** = healthy carrier example.

## 13. Virulence factors — the big idea 🎯
- Molecules that let a microbe **colonize, evade immunity, enter/exit, and get nutrients.**
- **Pathogenicity** = ability to cause disease; **virulence** = degree of it.
- Intrinsic (capsule, endotoxin) vs. plasmid-acquired (some toxins).

## 14. The 4 categories of virulence factors 🎯
- **(1) Structural** (mycolic acid, peptidoglycan, capsule, envelope, endospore, flagella, fimbriae —
  ⛔ details "you're well versed"), **(2) Toxins**, **(3) Enzymes**, **(4) Anti-phagocytic.** ("Multiple choice.")
- The **slide** lists five (adhesion factors · biofilms · extracellular enzymes · toxins · antiphagocytic);
  **adhesion & biofilms are "structural."**
- **Adhesion** *(Slide 31)* = attaching to host cells (adhesion factors, specialized structures like
  fimbriae, attachment proteins) — required to establish colonies. **Biofilm** = a protective surface
  community that helps a pathogen persist.

## 15. Toxins — exotoxins vs. endotoxins 🎯
- **Exotoxins:** secreted, protein, **heat-labile**, **very toxic** in tiny amounts, **no fever**; become
  **toxoids** (keep epitopes → **antitoxin/IgG**); cyto-/neuro-/enterotoxins. Botulism, tetanus, gangrene,
  diphtheria, cholera, plague, staph food poisoning. **Tetanospasmin** & **botulinum** = most potent.
- **Endotoxins:** **lipid A of LPS** in **Gram-negative** cell walls; **heat-stable**; **low toxicity but
  high numbers**; **fever (pyrogen)**, dehydration, **coagulation**, **septic shock**; **no vaccine.**
  Typhoid, tularemia, UTIs, meningococcal meningitis.
- **Clinical caution:** bursting many Gram-negatives at once releases more lipid A → patient can worsen.

## 16. Enzymes as virulence factors 🎯
- **Lipase** (breaks skin oleic acid → pimples), **coagulase** (clots plasma → abscess; *S. aureus*),
  **hyaluronidase** (destroys hyaluronic-acid "cement" → **spread factor**; strep pyogenes / staph aureus).

## 17. Anti-phagocytic factors 🎯
- **Protein A** (*S. aureus*) and **M protein** (*S. pyogenes*) block phagocytosis. Capsule counts too.

## 18. Recap of virulence factors 🎯
- Four categories again: **structural · toxins · enzymes · anti-phagocytic** — the easy way to remember them.

## 19. The body's defenses — three lines 🎯
- **Innate (non-specific):** 1st line = barriers; 2nd line = **interferon, phagocytosis, complement.**
- **Adaptive/acquired (specific):** 3rd line = **immunity/immunoglobulins**, **B & T lymphocytes** (next time).
- **Innate immunity** = nonspecific, acts immediately/within hours.

## 20. First line — physical & chemical barriers 🎯
- **Physical:** skin (largest organ; intact = protected), mucous membranes (ears, mouth, nose, anus, urethra).
- **Chemical:** sebum/oleic acid, **lysozyme** (tears/saliva), lactic acid/electrolytes in sweat, acidic
  skin pH, **HCl** in stomach, bile, semen, **acidic vaginal pH**.

## 21. Flushing mechanisms & mucociliary escalator 🎯
- Sneeze · cough · **lacrimation** · sweat · **cerumen (ear wax)** · salivation · defecation · **peristalsis.**
- **Mucociliary escalator:** ciliated tracheal/lung cells sweep debris up to the throat to be coughed out.

## 22. Diapedesis 🎯
- **Diapedesis** = WBCs moving out of the circulation into tissue to fight infection.
- Auto-transcript writes "diabetes" — *"be sure you know that term… it's an I, not a Y."*

## 23. Complement 🎯
- A **cascade of ≥26 proteins** ("like a sparkler"); **neutralizes viruses, enhances phagocytosis, damages
  the membrane, lyses microbes.** Pathways (reference): **classical · lectin · alternative.** Kept simple.

## 24. Interferon 🎯
- Made by **every cell** in tiny amounts; **antiviral & anti-cancer**; **interferes with transcription/
  translation of mRNA.** Types **alpha, beta, gamma.** **Betaseron** (IFN-β) for MS.

## 25. Normal flora & natural resistance 🎯
- **Normal flora** (commensal/mutualistic) out-competes pathogens for space/nutrients.
- **Natural resistance** acquired over life (and from mother in childhood).

## 26. Self vs. non-self & the immune system 🎯
- **Immunology** = 2nd & 3rd lines. Healthy system: **surveillance → recognition → destruction.**
- WBCs read **surface markers**; **non-self = antigen.** Every cell marked "you" **except RBCs** → transfusion.
- Transplant rejection → lifelong **immunosuppressants**.
