# Key Terms & Acronyms — Determinants of Health & Disease

All definitions are drawn from your professor's slides and lecture. Plain-language phrasing is mine; the
science is his.

## Acronyms (decode these instantly)

| Acronym | Full name | In one line |
|---|---|---|
| **HAI** | Healthcare-Associated Infection | Modern term for a **nosocomial** (healthcare-acquired) infection |
| **CAI** | Community-Acquired Infection | Infection acquired **outside** healthcare settings |
| **LPS** | Lipopolysaccharide | Gram-negative outer-wall molecule; its **lipid A** is the **endotoxin** |
| **HUS** | Hemolytic Uremic Syndrome | Serious *E. coli* O157:H7 complication (blood + kidneys; ~20% mortality) |
| **HPV** | Human Papillomavirus | Linked to **cervical** (and anal/oral) cancer |
| **CHD** | Cardiovascular Heart Disease | Example of a non-infectious **disease** |
| **IFN** | Interferon | Antiviral/anti-cancer protein made by every cell |
| **TSS** | Toxic Shock Syndrome | Caused by *S. aureus* **enterotoxin F** |
| **IV** | Intravenous | The main **parenteral** route for microbe entry (shared needles) |

## Determinants of health

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Physical health** | Measurable body health (blood chemistry, BP, BMI) |
| **Mental health** | Psychological health; believing you're unwell raises susceptibility |
| **Spiritual health** | Having a **purpose** (not necessarily religion); linked to better outcomes |
| **Social determinants of health** | Income, education, employment, environment, healthcare access, etc. |
| **Socioeconomic factors** | The **largest-impact** driver of health on the intervention pyramid |
| **Epigenetics** | Changes in gene expression (not DNA sequence) that shape disease risk |

## Pathogens & pathogenicity

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Pathogenic** | Able to **cause disease** |
| **Opportunistic pathogen** | Harmless in its normal habitat; pathogenic when the host is debilitated or it lands in the wrong site |
| **Virulent** | Having a **high degree** of ability to cause infection |
| **Pathogen (infectious agent / germ)** | A biological agent that causes disease in a host |
| **Pathogenicity** | The **ability** of a microbe to cause disease |
| **Virulence** | The **degree** of pathogenicity |
| **Virulence factor** | A molecule that helps a microbe colonize, evade immunity, enter/exit, and get nutrients |
| **Adhesion** | The process of attaching to host cells (via adhesion factors, fimbriae, attachment proteins) — needed to establish colonies |
| **Biofilm** | A surface-attached microbial community in a protective matrix; a structural virulence strategy |
| **Hemolytic uremic syndrome (HUS)** | *E. coli* O157:H7 complication — in the blood, attacks kidneys, ~20% mortality |

## Entry, contamination & infection

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Portal of entry** | Where a microbe enters the body (cuts, lesions, injection sites, orifices) |
| **Four portals of entry** | Skin · mucous membranes · placenta · parenteral route |
| **Parenteral route** | Entry that bypasses skin/mucosa — e.g., IV drug use / shared needles |
| **Contamination** | Microbes merely **present** on a surface or in a liquid (objects can be contaminated) |
| **Infection** | A microbe that has evaded defenses, multiplied, and become **established** in the body |
| **Reservoir** | A source that harbors a microbe (e.g., a contaminated cutting board) |
| **Bacteremia** | Bacteria in the bloodstream |
| **Infectious process** | Entry → growth → nutrient access; block any one and no infection develops |

## Disease & its classification

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Disease** | A harmful alteration of host tissue or metabolism (no microbe required) |
| **-itis** | Suffix = **inflammation**, usually from a microbe (cellulitis, pharyngitis, cystitis…) |
| **Etiology** | The study of the **cause** of a disease |
| **Germ theory** | Disease can be caused by infection with pathogenic microbes |
| **Koch's postulates** | Criteria that prove a specific microbe causes a specific disease |
| **Immunological (disease)** | Immune-system dysfunction — hypoactive or hyperactive (anaphylaxis) |
| **Neoplastic** | Tumor / cancer |
| **Iatrogenic** | Caused by a medical procedure or treatment |
| **Idiopathic** | Cause unknown |
| **Nosocomial** | Hospital-/healthcare-acquired (now called **HAI**) |
| **Oncogene** | A cancer-promoting gene some people carry from birth |

## Infection vs. intoxication

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Intoxication** | Illness from a **toxin** a microbe made — short, sharp, **no fever** |
| **Foodborne infection** | The **organism itself grows** in the body — longer, **with fever** |
| **Enterotoxin F** | *S. aureus* toxin that causes **toxic shock syndrome** |
| **Tenesmus** | The urge to defecate when nothing passes |
| **Carrier** | A healthy person who harbors and spreads a pathogen (e.g., **Typhoid Mary**) |

## Toxins

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Toxemia** | Toxins carried in the bloodstream beyond the infection site |
| **Exotoxin** | **Secreted** protein toxin; **heat-labile**; very toxic in tiny amounts; **no fever** |
| **Endotoxin** | **Lipid A of LPS** in the Gram-negative cell wall; **heat-stable**; needs high numbers; **fever** |
| **Toxoid** | A denatured (heat-treated) exotoxin that keeps its epitopes → used in vaccines |
| **Antitoxin** | IgG antibody against a toxin (e.g., tetanus/botulism vaccine, antivenom) |
| **Epitope** | The surface curvature of a protein that an antibody recognizes |
| **Tetanospasmin** | The tetanus exotoxin (*Clostridium tetani*) — one of the two most potent toxins |
| **Botulinum toxin** | *Clostridium botulinum* exotoxin — extraordinarily potent (a neurotoxin) |
| **Cytotoxin / neurotoxin / enterotoxin** | Exotoxins that kill cells / affect nerves / cause vomiting |
| **Septic (endotoxic) shock** | Life-threatening result of high endotoxin load |
| **Pyrogen** | A fever-inducing substance (endotoxins are pyrogens) |

## Enzymes & anti-phagocytic factors

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Extracellular enzyme** | Enzyme secreted by a pathogen to dissolve host structural chemicals |
| **Lipase** | Breaks down protective skin fatty acids (oleic acid) → pimples |
| **Coagulase** | Clots plasma into a fibrin wall around bacteria → abscess/boil (*S. aureus*) |
| **Hyaluronidase** | Destroys **hyaluronic acid** ("cell cement") → the **spread factor** |
| **Hyaluronic acid** | The substance that holds cells/tissues together |
| **Protein A** | Anti-phagocytic protein of *Staphylococcus aureus* |
| **M protein** | Anti-phagocytic protein of *Streptococcus pyogenes* |
| **Capsule** | A structural factor that also resists phagocytosis |

## Innate defenses

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Innate immunity** | Nonspecific defenses acting immediately/within hours of an antigen |
| **Three lines of defense** | 1st = barriers; 2nd = interferon/phagocytosis/complement; 3rd = adaptive immunity |
| **Physical barrier** | Skin and mucous membranes blocking entry |
| **Lysozyme** | Enzyme in tears/saliva that breaks down bacterial cell walls |
| **Sebum / oleic acid** | Antimicrobial skin secretions |
| **Flushing mechanism** | Physical washout of microbes (sneeze, cough, tears, sweat, defecation, etc.) |
| **Lacrimation** | Tearing — a flushing/protective mechanism for the eye |
| **Cerumen** | Ear wax — a protective secretion |
| **Peristalsis** | Movement of a food bolus through the GI tract |
| **Mucociliary escalator** | Ciliated airway cells sweeping debris/mucus up to the throat |
| **Diapedesis** | WBCs squeezing out of blood vessels into tissue to fight infection |
| **Complement** | Cascade of ≥26 proteins: neutralize viruses, enhance phagocytosis, damage membranes, lyse microbes |
| **Complement pathways** | Classical · lectin · alternative (all reach a similar end) |
| **Interferon (IFN)** | Antiviral/anti-cancer protein; blocks transcription/translation of mRNA (types α, β, γ) |
| **Betaseron** | Interferon-beta therapy used for MS flares |
| **Normal flora / microbiota** | Commensal/mutualistic organisms that out-compete pathogens |
| **Natural resistance** | Resistance acquired over a lifetime (and from mother in childhood) |

## Self, non-self & the immune system

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Immunology** | The study of the body's 2nd and 3rd lines of defense |
| **Surveillance / recognition / destruction** | The three jobs of a healthy immune system |
| **Self** | Cells marked as belonging to you (all cells except RBCs) |
| **Non-self = antigen** | Anything foreign that the immune system targets |
| **Surface marker** | The molecule WBCs read to tell self from non-self |
| **Immunosuppressant** | Drug taken for life so the body won't reject a (non-self) transplant |
| **B lymphocyte** | Runs **humoral** (antibody) immunity — detailed next unit |
| **T lymphocyte** | Runs **cell-mediated** immunity — detailed next unit |
| **Acquired (adaptive) immunity** | Specific, memory-based immunity built over a lifetime |
